# Mission: AI/ML in Banking & Finance

## Why
You are an AI/ML Engineer who already commands the core algorithm toolkit — from ensembles to transformers — and now wants to operate fluently inside the financial domain: knowing *why* a technique is chosen, *how* it is adapted to regulatory constraints, and *what* risks it introduces so you can govern it as a risk lead, not just build it as an engineer.

## Success looks like
- Explain, technically, how PD/LGD/EAD models are built with ML and where they improve on classical scorecards
- Articulate the model risk management (MRM) framework (SR 11-7 / SS1/23) and its implications for model development, validation, and governance
- Describe the ML approaches to Market Risk (VaR, FRTB-NMRF, stress testing) and why they outperform parametric methods
- Explain how Fraud and AML detection pipelines are designed, evaluated (recall/precision trade-off), and governed
- In a risk governance conversation, identify key controls and red flags for an ML model in production

## Constraints
- Already fluent in: logistic/linear regression, SVMs, decision trees, ensembles, deep learning, transformers/Gen AI
- Dual lens: every topic must be covered technically AND from a risk/governance angle
- Depth over breadth: prefer mastery of a few areas over survey coverage

## Out of scope
- Basic ML algorithm theory (already known)
- Pure quantitative finance (Black-Scholes, stochastic calculus) unless needed to contextualise an ML technique
- Python/implementation deep-dives unless directly tied to a finance use case
